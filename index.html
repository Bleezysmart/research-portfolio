Online Retail Sales & Returns Analysis

An end-to-end retail analytics project examining sales performance, returns behavior, revenue trends, and geographic concentration. Using Excel dashboards and KPI-driven reporting, the analysis identified key drivers of revenue, loss patterns, and operational risks within the business.

Key Insights:
• The business generated £13.2M in net revenue across the analysis period.
• Product returns resulted in £896.8K in lost revenue, representing a 6.78% loss rate.
• The United Kingdom accounted for 88% of total revenue, highlighting significant market concentration risk.
• Sales performance was driven by a relatively small group of high-value products and customer segments.

Netflix Content Analytics Dashboard

A business intelligence project analyzing 8,796 Netflix titles to understand content distribution, audience targeting, genre preferences, geographic footprint, and content acquisition strategy. The project involved data cleaning, transformation, exploratory analysis, dashboard development, and business reporting to uncover patterns within Netflix's content catalog.

Key Insights:
• Movies account for 69.7% of the catalog, significantly outweighing TV Shows.
• International Movies emerged as the largest content category with 2,752 titles.
• The United States contributed the highest number of titles (3,688), followed by India and the United Kingdom.
• 63.1% of content was acquired within two years of release, indicating a strong preference for newer content.

This research, originally developed for the Mitosis ecosystem, explores whether L2s remain a necessity in 2025 or if the industry shift toward unified interoperability and alternative scaling layers is rendering them obsolete. Technical Risk Mechanics: Liquidations & Insurance Funds

A deep dive into the architecture of exchange safety nets. I explain the flow of money during liquidations and why understanding these "zero-sum" mechanics is critical for assessing protocol-level risk and systemic stability in decentralized markets.

Key insight:
The Zero-Sum Dynamic: Explains that for every long, there is a short, highlighting that futures markets are a transfer of value based on risk appetite rather than asset creation.
Insurance Fund Architecture: Details how exchanges use insurance funds as a first line of defense to prevent "socialized losses," ensuring that winning traders are paid even when losers are liquidated beyond their collateral.
Liquidation as a Safety Valve: Reframes liquidations not as a failure, but as a necessary automated mechanism to protect the overall system's solvency and liquidity.
Risk Management Education: Advocates for data-driven trading by simplifying complex concepts like funding rates and maintenance margins for a broader audience.
